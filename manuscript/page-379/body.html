A voice and vision called him to be a priest,--a seer to lead the uncalled out of the house of bondage. He saw the headless host turn toward him like the whirling of mad waters,--he stretched forth his hands eagerly, and then, even as he stretched them, suddenly there swept across the vision the temptation of Despair.

They were not wicked men,--the problem of life is not the problem of the wicked,--they were calm, good men, Bishops of the Apostolic Church of God, and strove toward righteousness. They said slowly, "It is all very natural--it is even commendable; but the General Theological Seminary of the Episcopal Church cannot admit a Negro."
