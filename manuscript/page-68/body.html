The largest element of success lay in the fact that the majority of the freedmen were willing, even eager, to work. So labor contracts were written,--fifty thousand in a single State,--laborers advised, wages guaranteed, and employers supplied. In truth, the organization became a vast labor bureau,--not perfect, indeed, notably defective here and there, but on the whole successful beyond the dreams of thoughtful men. The two great obstacles which confronted the officials were the tyrant and the idler,--the slaveholder who was determined to perpetuate slavery under another name; and, the freedman who regarded freedom as perpetual rest,--the Devil and the Deep Sea.
