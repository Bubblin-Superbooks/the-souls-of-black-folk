"Own land?" said the wife; "well, only this house." Then she added quietly. "We did buy seven hundred acres across up yonder, and paid for it; but they cheated us out of it. Sells was the owner." "Sells!" echoed the ragged misfortune, who was leaning against the balustrade and listening, "he's a regular cheat. I worked for him thirty-seven days this spring, and he paid me in cardboard checks which were to be cashed at the end of the month. But he never cashed them,--kept putting me off. Then the sheriff came and took my mule and corn and furniture--" "Furniture? But furniture is exempt from seizure by law." "Well, he took it just the same," said the hard-faced man.
